== What Step 1 Captures

The first heartbeat of the particle concept: *discovered, lost, rediscovered, lost again at the first crisis.* One complete cycle.

Everything after Thomson (1897) -- Rutherford, Bohr, Chadwick, Dirac, Wigner, quarks, Higgs -- is post-crisis work. Not in this document. Steps 2-4.

The deliverable is the *concept* of particle, traced through the minds that built it, ending at the wound. Biographies are how the concept grew. The concept is the protagonist.

== North Stars

Things Dad already signaled:

- LIKED: "particle was separated from divinity and concept took off with Newton." -- Newton is the hinge.
- LIKED: reasoning from attributes -- "how would spin and vector make a wave?"
- DISLIKED: quark detours, Higgs talk, modern particle zoo. Wrong step.
- RULE: short with understanding > long with slop.

Match the tightness of your Gödel piece. Aim ~3-4 pages of prose. Every paragraph either advances the concept or it doesn't belong.

== Thesis (write last, state first)

State in 2-3 sentences what the concept of particle *was* at the moment Thomson cracked it. The pre-crisis spine. Stub for you to rewrite in your own words:

[NOTE]
====
A particle is an irreducible, indivisible carrier of identity, with quantitative attributes (mass, position, weight), conserved through interactions, that combines with others in fixed proportions to constitute all matter.
====

Drop this in once the body is written. It will probably sharpen as you go.

== Phase 1: Discovery -- Democritus (5th c BCE)

One question for each thinker: *what was the concept doing in their head, and what did they add to it?*

Prompts:

- What problem was Democritus answering? (Parmenides: change is impossible.)
- What did the concept let him do that Parmenides couldn't? (Change as rearrangement.)
- What two things did he introduce? (Atoms + void.)
- Which of his own words still survives? ("By convention sweet, by convention bitter... in reality, atoms and void.")

One tight paragraph. The concept here is *philosophical, not yet mathematical.*

=== Draft v0 (Claude's navigation pass -- rewrite in your own voice)

[NOTE]
====
The concept is born answering Parmenides. If nothing can come from nothing and nothing can become nothing, then change itself is impossible -- and yet the world plainly changes. Democritus (c. 460-370 BCE), extending Leucippus, splits reality in two: _atoms_, the irreducible carriers of being, and _void_, the nothing through which they move. Change becomes rearrangement. Nothing is created and nothing destroyed; what differs from one moment to the next is only how the atoms stand to one another. Already the concept carries the attributes it will be remembered by -- indivisibility, eternity, difference by shape and size -- but it carries them as philosophy, not as mathematics. Democritus's own words survive him almost intact: _"By convention sweet, by convention bitter, by convention hot, by convention cold, by convention color: but in reality atoms and void."_ The concept is awake. It will sleep for nearly two thousand years.
====

Things to push on:

- Is "irreducible carriers of being" already too metaphysical-sounding, or right for a philosophical phase?
- "Nearly two thousand years" -- foreshadow into Phase 2 or cut and let Phase 2 do its own work?
- Do you want Leucippus in the spine at all, or is Democritus the only name that earns its keep here?

== Phase 2: Loss -- Aristotle to Lucretius (~1800 years)

Brief. One or two paragraphs. The concept is buried but not killed.

Prompts:

- Why did Aristotle's continuum view win? (Teleological, purposive, fit Christianity later.)
- How did the concept survive? (Epicurus → Lucretius → _De Rerum Natura_, surviving in *one manuscript*, rediscovered 1417.)
- What does this long silence teach about abstractions? (They can sleep for centuries and still be true.)

Don't dwell. The point: alive, then quiet, then revived.

== Phase 3: Rediscovery -- Gassendi → Newton → Dalton

The meat of step 1. Three thinkers, same concept, different work.

=== Gassendi (1592-1655)

Prompts:

- What did he solve? (Theological -- atoms after Galileo's trial.)
- What did he add? (Atoms safe for Christians.)
- Why does he matter even though he wasn't a physicist? (Without him, no Newton.)

One paragraph.

=== Newton (1643-1727) -- the hinge

This is the most important section of step 1. Give it weight.

Prompts:

- What did Newton *strip* from the concept? (Metaphysics, purpose, divine cause.)
- What did he replace it with? (Math -- point mass, position vector, force law.)
- Why does math make the concept eternal? (Computable, predictive, no longer dependent on philosophy.)
- What does stem:[F = ma] *do* to the concept? (Particles become variables in equations. The world becomes calculable.)

This is where Dad's line lives: *"particle was separated from divinity, and the concept took off."* Say it in your own words. Anchor the section here.

One to two paragraphs.

=== Dalton (1766-1844)

Prompts:

- What problem was he solving? (Why elements combine in fixed proportions.)
- What did he add? (Each kind of atom has a *number* -- atomic weight.)
- How does this change the concept? (Chemistry becomes counting. Atoms have measurable, distinguishing attributes.)
- What did NOT survive him? (His "indivisible" claim -- though he still asserted it.)

One paragraph.

== Phase 4: First Crisis -- Thomson (1897)

Prompts:

- What did Thomson do? (Cathode rays. Measured stem:[e/m]. 1000× lighter than hydrogen. Universal across cathode materials.)
- What did the result mean? (Atoms have internal structure. There's something inside.)
- Which defining attribute of the concept dies here? (*Indivisibility.* The word _atomos_ -- "uncuttable" -- is falsified by name.)
- What does NOT die? (The deeper structure: irreducible carrier of attributes, conserved, combining in fixed ways. Just at a smaller scale now.)

One or two paragraphs. Stop at the moment of the wound. *Do not* mention Rutherford, Bohr, plum pudding's fall, or anything after. This is where step 1 ends.

== Closing: The Concept at the End of the First Cycle

Two moves:

1. State what the concept *is* at this moment -- wounded but not dead. The surface attribute (indivisibility) is gone. The deeper structure survives.
2. Foreshadow without explaining: the concept will be rediscovered more deeply in the next cycle. The first crisis ends the surface form, not the abstraction. *One sentence.* No QM, no Wigner, no rep theory. Save it for step 3.

== Drafting Rules

- Tightness of the Gödel piece. ~3-4 pages. No more.
- Each thinker = one paragraph. Newton gets two.
- Cut every name and date that isn't load-bearing. If removing a fact doesn't weaken the spine, the fact doesn't belong.
- The biographies are not the goal. The concept's life *through* those minds is the goal.
- End at Thomson. Not one paragraph past.
