= 10th Grade Math Cheat Sheet
:toc: left
:toclevels: 2
:icons: font
:stem: latexmath

A reconciliation map between Algebra 1 (the foundation you should already own) and Algebra 2 (where you're actually headed). Use Algebra 1 as a diagnostic -- don't re-grind what's already solid.

TIP: Each topic has a *Practice* block. Try them before peeking at any solutions -- getting stuck is where the learning happens. If a topic feels trivial, skip the drills and move on.

== Algebra 1 -- Foundation

Verify with a diagnostic. Anything shaky here will hurt in Algebra 2.

=== Foundations
Variables, expressions, order of operations, properties of real numbers.

.Practice
. Evaluate: stem:[3 + 4 \cdot (2^2 - 5) + 6 \div 2].
. Simplify: stem:[5(2x - 3) - 2(x - 7)].
. Name the property used: stem:[a + (b + c) = (a + b) + c].
. Evaluate stem:[x^3 - 4x + 1] when stem:[x = -2].
. Insert parentheses to make the statement true: stem:[8 - 3 \cdot 2 + 1 = 5].

=== Linear Equations & Inequalities
Solving in one variable.

.Practice
. Solve: stem:[3(x - 4) = 2x + 5].
. Solve: stem:[\frac{x}{3} - \frac{x}{4} = 2].
. Solve and graph on a number line: stem:[-2x + 7 \geq 15].
. Solve: stem:[|2x - 1| < 5].
. A taxi charges \$3 plus \$2 per mile. How many miles correspond to a \$19 fare? Set up and solve an equation.

=== Linear Functions & Graphing
Slope, intercepts, and the forms of a line (slope-intercept, point-slope, standard).

.Practice
. Find the slope of the line through stem:[(-1, 4)] and stem:[(3, -2)].
. Write the equation (slope-intercept form) of the line through stem:[(2, -1)] with slope stem:[3].
. Convert stem:[2x - 3y = 12] to slope-intercept form.
. Find the line parallel to stem:[y = \tfrac{1}{2}x + 4] passing through stem:[(4, 1)].
. A line passes through stem:[(0, -3)] and stem:[(5, 7)]. Write its equation in standard form.

=== Systems of Linear Equations
Substitution, elimination, graphing.

.Practice
. Solve by substitution: stem:[y = 2x + 1] and stem:[3x + y = 11].
. Solve by elimination: stem:[2x + 3y = 12] and stem:[4x - y = 10].
. Two numbers sum to 30 and differ by 8. Find them.
. Solve: stem:[x + y + z = 6], stem:[2x - y + z = 3], stem:[x + 2y - z = 5].
. How many solutions does the system stem:[4x - 2y = 6], stem:[2x - y = 3] have? Justify.

=== Exponents
Exponent rules and manipulation.

.Practice
. Simplify: stem:[(3x^2 y^3)(2x^4 y)].
. Simplify: stem:[\dfrac{a^5 b^2}{a^2 b^5}].
. Evaluate: stem:[2^{-3} + 3^0].
. Simplify: stem:[(2x^3)^4].
. Solve for stem:[n]: stem:[2^n = 32].

=== Polynomials
Adding, subtracting, multiplying.

.Practice
. Add: stem:[(3x^2 - 2x + 1) + (x^2 + 4x - 6)].
. Multiply: stem:[(2x - 3)(x + 5)].
. Expand: stem:[(x - 4)^2].
. Subtract: stem:[(5x^3 - x + 2) - (2x^3 + 3x^2 - 1)].
. Multiply: stem:[(x + 2)(x^2 - 3x + 4)].

=== Factoring
The big one. GCF, trinomials, difference of squares.

.Practice
. Factor: stem:[x^2 + 7x + 12].
. Factor: stem:[x^2 - 49].
. Factor: stem:[6x^2 + 11x - 10].
. Factor by grouping: stem:[x^3 - 2x^2 + 3x - 6].
. Factor completely: stem:[3x^3 - 27x].

=== Quadratic Equations & Functions
Factoring, the quadratic formula, parabolas.

.Practice
. Solve by factoring: stem:[x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0].
. Solve with the quadratic formula: stem:[2x^2 + 3x - 5 = 0].
. Find the vertex of stem:[y = x^2 - 6x + 11].
. A ball follows stem:[h(t) = -16t^2 + 32t + 6]. When does it hit the ground?
. Use the discriminant to determine how many real roots stem:[3x^2 - 4x + 2 = 0] has.

=== Radicals & Rational Expressions
Basic operations and simplification.

.Practice
. Simplify: stem:[\sqrt{72}].
. Rationalize: stem:[\dfrac{3}{\sqrt{5}}].
. Simplify: stem:[\dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x^2 - x - 6}].
. Add: stem:[\dfrac{1}{x} + \dfrac{2}{x + 1}].
. Solve: stem:[\sqrt{2x + 3} = 5].

=== Intro to Data & Statistics
Sometimes tacked on at the end.

.Practice
. Find the mean, median, and mode of: stem:[4, 7, 7, 9, 12, 15].
. Compute the range and the interquartile range of the data above.
. A class has mean stem:[80], standard deviation stem:[5]. A student scored stem:[90]. How many standard deviations above the mean is she?
. A bag has 3 red and 5 blue marbles. What is the probability of drawing red?
. Plot stem:[(1,2), (2,4), (3,5), (4,8)] on a scatter plot. Describe the correlation and estimate a line of best fit.

== Algebra 2 -- Where You're Headed

=== Equations & Inequalities Review
Fast pass -- Algebra 1 carryover.

.Practice
. Solve: stem:[5 - 2(3x - 4) = 7].
. Solve: stem:[|3x - 2| = 7].
. Solve: stem:[2x^2 - 5x - 3 = 0].
. Solve the inequality: stem:[x^2 - x - 6 < 0].
. Solve the system: stem:[2x + y = 5] and stem:[x^2 + y = 7].

=== Functions
Notation, domain/range, transformations, inverses, composition.

.Practice
. If stem:[f(x) = 2x - 3] and stem:[g(x) = x^2], find stem:[(f \circ g)(2)].
. Find the inverse of stem:[f(x) = \dfrac{x - 4}{3}].
. State the domain of stem:[f(x) = \dfrac{\sqrt{x - 2}}{x - 5}].
. Describe the transformations taking stem:[f(x) = x^2] to stem:[g(x) = -2(x + 3)^2 + 1].
. If stem:[f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x - 2}], find stem:[f(f(3))].

=== Quadratics & Complex Numbers
Imaginary numbers enter here.

.Practice
. Simplify: stem:[(3 + 2i)(4 - i)].
. Solve: stem:[x^2 + 4x + 13 = 0].
. Express in standard form: stem:[\dfrac{2 + i}{1 - i}].
. Find the modulus stem:[|3 - 4i|].
. Solve: stem:[x^2 = -16].

=== Polynomial Functions
Higher-degree polynomials, polynomial division, roots, factor and remainder theorems.

.Practice
. Use long division: stem:[(2x^3 - 3x^2 + x - 5) \div (x - 2)].
. Use the Remainder Theorem to find stem:[P(-1)] for stem:[P(x) = x^4 - 3x^2 + 2].
. Find all rational roots of stem:[x^3 - 2x^2 - 5x + 6].
. Describe the end behavior of stem:[f(x) = -2x^5 + 3x^2 - 1].
. Write a polynomial of degree 4 (real coefficients) with roots stem:[1], stem:[-2], and stem:[3i].

=== Rational Expressions & Functions
Asymptotes show up.

.Practice
. Simplify: stem:[\dfrac{x^2 - 4}{x^2 + 4x + 4}].
. Find all asymptotes of stem:[f(x) = \dfrac{2x^2 - 3}{x^2 - 1}].
. Solve: stem:[\dfrac{1}{x - 1} + \dfrac{2}{x} = 3].
. Multiply and simplify: stem:[\dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x + 2} \cdot \dfrac{x + 2}{x - 3}].
. Identify any holes vs vertical asymptotes in stem:[f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - x - 6}{x - 3}].

=== Radical Functions & Rational Exponents
Connecting roots to fractional exponents.

.Practice
. Simplify: stem:[x^{1/2} \cdot x^{2/3}].
. Solve: stem:[\sqrt{x + 7} = x - 5] (check for extraneous solutions).
. Rewrite stem:[x^{3/4}] in radical form.
. State the domain of stem:[f(x) = \sqrt{2x - 6}].
. Simplify: stem:[\sqrt[3]{54 x^4}].

=== Exponential Functions
Growth, decay, base-_e_.

.Practice
. Solve: stem:[3^{x+1} = 81].
. A population doubles every 5 years. If it is 1,000 now, what is it in 20 years?
. Sketch stem:[f(x) = 2^x] and stem:[g(x) = (1/2)^x] on the same axes; compare.
. Compute the value of \$1,000 invested at 5% continuous interest for 10 years using stem:[A = Pe^{rt}].
. Solve: stem:[e^{2x} = 7].

=== Logarithms
The classic wall. Budget extra time.

.Practice
. Evaluate: stem:[\log_2 32].
. Solve: stem:[\log(x) + \log(x - 3) = 1].
. Use change of base to evaluate stem:[\log_5 20].
. Expand: stem:[\log\!\left(\dfrac{x^2 y^3}{z}\right)].
. Solve: stem:[5^x = 12].

=== Sequences & Series
Arithmetic, geometric, summation notation.

.Practice
. Find the 20th term of the sequence stem:[3, 7, 11, 15, \ldots].
. Find the sum of the first 15 terms of stem:[2, 6, 18, 54, \ldots].
. Write in summation notation: stem:[1 + 4 + 9 + 16 + 25].
. Find the sum of the infinite series stem:[1 + \tfrac{1}{3} + \tfrac{1}{9} + \cdots].
. Prove that stem:[0.\overline{9} = 1] using a geometric series.

=== Conic Sections
Circles, ellipses, parabolas, hyperbolas.

.Practice
. Write the equation of the circle with center stem:[(-2, 3)] and radius stem:[5].
. Identify the conic and its key features: stem:[x^2 + 4y^2 = 16].
. Find the foci of stem:[\dfrac{x^2}{25} - \dfrac{y^2}{9} = 1].
. Convert to standard form: stem:[x^2 + y^2 - 6x + 4y - 12 = 0].
. Find the focus and directrix of stem:[y = \tfrac{1}{8}x^2].

=== Probability & Statistics
Often the closing unit.

.Practice
. A fair coin is flipped 4 times. What is the probability of exactly 3 heads?
. In how many ways can 5 distinct books be arranged on a shelf?
. Two dice are rolled. What is the probability the sum is 7?
. From a class of 20 students, how many ways can a committee of 3 be chosen?
. Test scores are normally distributed with stem:[\mu = 70], stem:[\sigma = 10]. What is the probability a score exceeds 85? (Use a z-table.)

=== Trigonometry Intro
Some Algebra 2 courses include the basics; others push it to Pre-Calc.

.Practice
. In a right triangle with legs 3 and 4, find stem:[\sin], stem:[\cos], and stem:[\tan] of the smaller acute angle.
. Convert stem:[150°] to radians.
. Evaluate: stem:[\sin(\pi/6)], stem:[\cos(\pi/4)], stem:[\tan(\pi/3)].
. In a 30-60-90 triangle with hypotenuse 10, find the two legs.
. In a right triangle, one acute angle is stem:[35°] and the opposite side is 12. Find the adjacent side (use a calculator).

== Resources -- How To Actually Learn This

Picked for depth over polish. Khan Academy is here for gap-checking only -- it's procedural and shallow on the _why_. The rest treat you like a mathematician.

=== Video Courses

[cols="2,1,3a", options="header"]
|===
| Resource | Cost | Why this one

| *Professor Leonard* -- Intermediate & College Algebra (YouTube)
| Free
| Full lecture series. Derives everything, no hand-waving. Best single video resource for Algebra 2 end-to-end.

| *3Blue1Brown* -- Lockdown Math, Essence of Linear Algebra, complex number videos
| Free
| Geometric intuition behind symbol manipulation. Watch his logarithm and complex number videos when those walls hit.

| *MIT OCW -- Highlights of Calculus* (Gilbert Strang)
| Free
| Strang explains structure, not steps. Shows algebra in service of higher math.

| *Khan Academy* -- Algebra 1 & 2
| Free
| Use as a diagnostic and fluency drill. Not a primary text -- it's procedural.
|===

=== Textbooks

[cols="2,1,3a", options="header"]
|===
| Resource | Cost | Why this one

| *Gelfand -- Algebra*
| ~$25
| Slim, rigorous, every problem earns its place. Written by a Fields-tier mathematician for serious students. Start here.

| *Gelfand -- Functions and Graphs* + *The Method of Coordinates*
| ~$20 each
| Companions to _Algebra_. Build the geometric half -- functional thinking, conics, coordinate geometry -- properly.

| *Lang -- Basic Mathematics*
| ~$50
| Algebra 1 through pre-calc as one unified rigorous whole. Lang writes algebra the way he'd write it for future mathematicians.

| *AoPS -- Introduction to Algebra* + *Intermediate Algebra* (Rusczyk)
| ~$60 each
| The gold standard for above-grade-level students. Full Algebra 1+2 coverage with problems that force genuine thinking.
|===
