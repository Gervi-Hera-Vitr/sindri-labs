== Math

=== Algebra II
Functions (linear, quadratic, polynomial, rational, exponential, logarithmic), systems of equations, matrices basics, sequences/series, complex numbers, conic sections, combinatorics.

=== Geometry
Triangle congruence/similarity, circles, polygons, area/volume, coordinate geometry, transformations, geometric proofs.

=== Trigonometry
Unit circle, six trig functions, identities, inverse trig, law of sines/cosines, radians, graphing.

=== Pre-Calculus
Function composition/transformation, limits intro, vectors, polar coordinates, parametric equations.

=== Statistics
Mean/median/SD/variance, normal and binomial distributions, z-scores, correlation vs causation, basic regression, hypothesis testing intro.

== Biology

Cell structure (prokaryote/eukaryote, organelles), membrane transport, mitosis/meiosis, macromolecules (carbs/lipids/proteins/nucleic acids), enzymes, ATP, DNA replication/transcription/translation, Mendelian genetics, mutations, evolution by natural selection, Hardy-Weinberg, ecology (food webs, energy flow, biogeochemical cycles), human body systems overview.

== Chemistry

Atomic structure, electron configuration, periodic trends (electronegativity, atomic radius, ionization energy), ionic/covalent/metallic bonding, intermolecular forces, stoichiometry (moles, balancing, limiting reagents, percent yield), reaction types (synthesis, decomposition, replacement, combustion, redox), gas laws, solutions and molarity, acid-base chemistry and pH, titration, thermodynamics basics (enthalpy, exo/endothermic).

== Physics
NOTE: Requires trigonometry first.

Kinematics, Newton's laws, forces (friction, tension, normal), work-energy theorem, conservation of energy, momentum, collisions, circular motion, gravitation basics, intro to waves and sound, intro to electricity/circuits.

== English / Writing

=== Reading
Shakespeare (start: _Macbeth_ or _Julius Caesar_), 19th-century novel (Austen, Twain, Dickens), 20th-century (Orwell, Steinbeck, Hemingway), one Russian (Dostoevsky short, or Chekhov stories), modern non-fiction argument.

=== Analysis
Theme, symbolism, narrative structure, point of view, irony, characterization.

=== Writing Modes
Analytical essay, argumentative essay, comparative essay, research paper with citations, narrative.

=== Mechanics
Advanced grammar (parallel structure, subjunctive, dangling modifiers), MLA format, transitions, paragraph structure.

=== Rhetoric
Ethos/pathos/logos, claim-evidence-warrant, common fallacies.

=== Vocabulary
SAT-level word list, Latin/Greek roots.

== History

=== World
Ancient civilizations (Mesopotamia, Egypt, Greece, Rome), medieval period, Renaissance, Reformation, Enlightenment, Industrial Revolution, WWI, WWII, Cold War, post-1991.

=== US
Colonial, Revolution, Constitution, Civil War, Reconstruction, Progressive Era, world wars, Civil Rights, modern.

=== Civics
Constitution, three branches, federalism, electoral system, Bill of Rights.

=== Economics
Supply/demand, marginal thinking, GDP, inflation, monetary vs fiscal policy, comparative advantage, market vs planned systems, basics of finance (interest, risk, diversification).

=== Geography
World map literacy, major physical features, capitals, basic demographics.

== Foreign Language
TIP: Swedish recommended for Nordic relocation target.

Pronunciation and alphabet, ~2000 highest-frequency words (covers 80%+ of speech), present/past/future tenses, articles (en/ett), adjective agreement, V2 word order, daily comprehensible input (podcasts, YouTube), regular speaking practice.

== Computer Science
NOTE: Python.

Data structures (arrays, hashmaps, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graph basics), algorithms (sorting, searching, recursion, intro to dynamic programming), Big-O notation, object-oriented programming concepts, Git/version control, command line / Linux basics, HTTP and web fundamentals, ideally a second language (Python -- different paradigm from JS, useful for data/ML later).

== Reasoning & Meta-skills

Propositional logic, validity vs soundness, common logical fallacies, probability intuitions (base rates, Bayes' theorem, regression to the mean), cognitive biases (anchoring, availability, confirmation, sunk cost), epistemology basics, forecasting and calibration, formal note-taking method (Cornell or Zettelkasten), active recall and spaced repetition as defaults.
