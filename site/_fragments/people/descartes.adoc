== Slop

The one who said no to the void. Where the atomists split the world into atoms and empty space, Descartes denied that empty space could exist at all:
matter went all the way down, packed without gaps, and motion happened as swirling vortices in a completely full universe -- a _plenum_.
He is the great opponent in this story, the mechanical philosophy without the void. He also handed mathematics a tool it still uses every day:
analytic geometry, algebra and geometry fused onto a coordinate grid -- the "Cartesian" plane.

.Plenum versus void:
[%collapsible]
====
Leucippus invented atoms-plus-void to let change happen: things move because there is empty space to move into. Descartes refused the empty space.
For him extension just *is* matter, so a truly empty space is a contradiction -- the cosmos is packed solid, and motion is matter shoving matter in great whirlpools (vortices),
which is how he explained the orbits of the planets. The void-versus-plenum argument ran for a century; Torricelli's barometer and Newton's mechanics eventually decided it for the void --
but Descartes forced the atomists to actually defend the emptiness their whole picture depended on.
====
