= Dirac, "The Quantum Theory of the Electron" (1928)

// tag::citation[]
P.A.M. Dirac, "The Quantum Theory of the Electron," _Proceedings of the Royal Society A_ 117, 610 (1928).
// end::citation[]

// tag::annotation[]
The paper that predicted antimatter from pure mathematics, four years before Anderson found the positron. Dirac's mathematical-beauty criterion in action.
// end::annotation[]

== Context

Dirac combined quantum mechanics with special relativity to describe the electron. The equation worked but produced negative-energy solutions with no obvious physical interpretation. Most physicists would have waved them off as artifact. Dirac instead proposed they describe a real particle -- same mass as the electron, opposite charge.

Pure mathematical prediction. No experiment had hinted at such a particle. Anderson confirmed it in 1932.

Nobel Prize in Physics: 1933, shared with Schrödinger.
